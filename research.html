Password Recovery Using Partitioned Grover's Algorithm

Grover's algorithm offers a quadratic speedup over classical brute-force search, which makes it a credible long-term threat to password-based authentication schemes including bcrypt, PBKDF2, and Argon2. The barrier to practical use on current hardware is significant: restricted qubit counts and short coherence windows make full-circuit execution infeasible for any realistic password length.

This work introduces a partitioned Grover framework that addresses the hardware gap directly. The password is divided into two parts: a quantum part searched by Grover's algorithm and a classical part enumerated by a coordinator process. A formal threshold-based detection criterion separates correct from incorrect classical partitions. The framework scales across partition sizes of m = 3, 4, 5, and 6 qubits, with simulated success probabilities within 0.001 of theoretical predictions for all tested configurations. Under a fixed five-qubit hardware budget, the partitioned circuit handles passwords up to seven bits, while the regular Grover circuit becomes infeasible beyond n = 5.

The implementation is built entirely in Qiskit 2.3.1 with noise modeling via qiskit-aer and a synthetic IBM-realistic noise model using GenericBackendV2. The noise analysis is documented transparently in the paper.

QAOA-Based Multi-Hop Relay Path Selection Using Fade Duration

Cooperative multi-hop relaying extends coverage in 6G networks, and relay path selection directly determines end-to-end reliability. Conventional approaches use signal-to-noise ratio as the link quality measure and either a greedy or exhaustive search as the solver. SNR captures average channel strength but says nothing about the temporal duration of fade events, which drives burst-error behavior in delay-sensitive traffic.

This paper replaces SNR with average fade duration (AFD) as the edge metric and solves the resulting combinatorial problem with a quantum approximate optimization algorithm (QAOA). The relay path selection problem is cast as a quadratic unconstrained binary optimization (QUBO) and solved with a depth-two QAOA circuit over an eight-qubit, two-tier decode-and-forward relay network. Results show that QAOA with AFD closely tracks the optimum exhaustive search and reduces total path AFD by up to 37% compared to conventional SNR-based selection. A key finding is that the choice of edge metric matters more than solver quality: the right metric with a near-optimal solver consistently outperforms the wrong metric with an optimal one.

RSMA-Enabled RIS-Assisted Integrated Sensing and Communication for 6G: A Comprehensive Survey

Next-generation wireless systems are expected to handle sensing and communication jointly on a shared hardware platform. This survey examines how three enabling technologies, rate-splitting multiple access (RSMA), reconfigurable intelligent surfaces (RIS), and integrated sensing and communication (ISAC), can work together in 6G deployments.

RSMA provides a flexible interference management framework that sits between treating interference as noise and fully decoding it. RIS introduces a programmable propagation environment through passive reflecting elements. ISAC enables simultaneous data transmission and environmental sensing without dedicated hardware for each function. The survey covers fundamental principles, mathematical models, and performance metrics for combined RSMA-RIS-ISAC systems. A comparative analysis of 20 recent works demonstrates that RSMA consistently outperforms NOMA and SDMA in spectral efficiency, energy efficiency, and sensing accuracy. The role of machine learning in addressing the non-convex optimization challenges in these systems is also examined, along with open research directions toward practical deployment.
